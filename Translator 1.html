About Baybayin

Baybayin is an ancient Filipino script where each symbol represents a syllable. It was used to write Tagalog and other languages of the Philippines prior to Spanish colonization. Each character has an inherent 'a' sound, with marks (kudlit) added to change vowels.

Examples

Filipino to Baybayin:

"Ako" → ᜀᜃᜓ

"Bayan" → ᜊᜌᜈ᜔

"Salamat" → ᜐᜎᜋᜆ᜔

"Pilipinas" → ᜉᜒᜎᜒᜉᜒᜈᜐ᜔

Baybayin to Filipino:

ᜀᜃᜓ → "Ako"

ᜃᜒᜆ → "Kita"

ᜋᜊᜓᜑᜌ᜔ → "Mabuhay"

ᜋᜄᜈ᜔ᜇ → "Maganda"

Lessons

Welcome to our Baybayin Lessons! Here, you can learn the basics of the ancient Filipino script step-by-step.

Lesson 1: Introduction to Baybayin Characters

Start with the basic characters representing syllables like "ba," "ka," "ma." Each character has an inherent 'a' sound.

Lesson 2: Adding Kudlit

Learn how to modify vowels using kudlit (dots or marks) to change 'a' to 'e,' 'i,' 'o,' or 'u.'

Lesson 3: Writing Simple Words

Practice writing common Filipino words such as "ako" (I), "bayan" (town), and "salamat" (thank you).

About Us

We are a group of college students from the Philippines passionate about preserving our cultural heritage. BaybayinPH is our project to make the ancient Baybayin script accessible to everyone.

As students, we’re learning and growing while building this tool, aiming to educate others about Filipino history and language. Our team consists of programmers, designers, and language enthusiasts working together to promote Baybayin in the digital age.
